## Welcome to the **PowerMyth Index!**

**How do we start to address social issues of our time? Inequity? Alienation? Division?** Maybe we already have the answers and just need to piece them together.

This digital garden attempts to craft a *universal contact language*. I believe deep division in society can be overcome, because there's something we are all shaped by and that we can all generate: **[[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]]**. Unfortunately, it's not as intuitive as we might expect. Power's relation to each individual, community, and identity manifests differently. The unique experiences everyone carries with them are all necessary for a greater understanding of Power, and therefore, each other.

A collective effort is needed because we're all partially blinded by **[[20 - 29 Myths/20 Power Myths/20.00. Power Myths\|Myth]]** of our own making. The stories we tell to understand the world around us are a veiled potential for Power, as they eventually become the unstated, the taken-for-granted, and the assumptions that inform our decision-making. What if those stories were written from a limited perspective? Individually, we might not know what to question, but together, we can **QUESTION EVERYTHING.**

## Contents
It should be no secret that the PowerMyth Index is interested in how different people experience and rationalize the world. I understand that not everyone, especially these days, sees diversity as a solution for inequity. In fact, sometimes even the smallest shows of diversity (a woke Disney movie casting or whatever) are branded with the scariest possible term man could imagine: ***Cultural Marxism***.

Okay, well look. I think collective identities like race and gender *do* have a lot to share about how Power (and therefore society) works. At the same time, the scholarly contributions now strawmanned as Cultural Marxism *did* have room to grow. They were challenged, and rightfully so. What about *normal* Americans?! What about the wealthy?! WHAT ABOUT GAMERS?!?!? Perhaps it's time for an update. The PowerMyth Index is organized following an epic new framework I like to call...

***NEW CULTURAL MARXISM+***

To get situated with the history of intellectual debate that informed this Index, head to the [[80 - 89 Resources/80 Thought/80.00. Bibliography\|Bibliography]] and Resources section. Otherwise, it should be understood as the following:

Understanding [[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]] solely as a means for people to dominate each other is insufficient. Instead...
- Power is a ubiquitous force that determines the boundaries for what every person can or can't do ([[10 - 19 Power/11. Realms of Freedom\|11. Realms of Freedom]]).
- When we come together to govern a society, the views and perceptions of some people are more represented than others ([[10 - 19 Power/12. Dominant Realm\|12. Dominant Realm]]).
- That influence is baked into institutions and culture, which maintains the status quo and reinforces exclusion ([[10 - 19 Power/13. Realm Enforcers\|13. Realm Enforcers]]).
- Those advantaged with accumulative properties, such as wealth and status, have greater potential to shape boundaries ([[10 - 19 Power/14. Power Capacitors\|14. Power Capacitors]]).
- An eternal process of [[10 - 19 Power/15. Boundary Negotiation\|15. Boundary Negotiation]] underpins society to determine what is good, possible, normal, legal, or rational.

## Fun Disclaimers

Instead, this Index will be **WEIRD** AND **MESSY** FOREVER!!! :O Ideas will keep evolving over time. Expect some pages to be delicately crafted essays while others will just be reference lists for now. Either way, my hope is that each page offers something you can chew on to help reexamine your own life experiences.
