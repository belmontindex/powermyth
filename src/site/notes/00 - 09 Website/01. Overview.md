## Welcome to the **PowerMyth Index!**

**How do we start to address social issues of our time? Inequity? Alienation? Division?** It's complicated... but maybe not really? The answers are already out there.

There's something we're all shaped by and that we can all generate: **[[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]]**. Its mechanisms determine what we can or cannot do, so a clear picture of those mechanisms could give us something to work with. However, Power isn't super intuitive when it manifests differently for every individual. AND we're partially blinded by **[[20 - 29 Myths/20 Power Myths/20.00. Power Myths\|Myth]]** of our own making... The stories we tell to rationalize reality eventually become the unstated, the taken-for-granted, and the assumptions that inform our decision-making. What if those stories were written from a limited perspective?

Welp, looks like we need a collective effort to build out Power as a *universal contact language*. Everyone's unique experiences can fill gaps and help us understand the world around us. Individually, we might not know what to question, but together, we can **QUESTION EVERYTHING.**

## Contents
People have been critiquing society since society existed *([[60 - 69 Power Generators/61 Countercultures/61.01. Gnosticism\|like, seriously]])*. However, inequity is deeply embedded across policy areas and experienced uniquely across identity. As knowledge becomes more specialized then, these contributions risk getting siloed and obscuring patterns. That's not super helpful for our complex, interconnected systems... but it's not reasonable to write some singularly unifying theory to bring it all together, either! Maybe we meet somewhere in the middle?

Power as a shared language allows us to roughly categorize relevant works from perspectives across fields of study, including those that disagree with one another. It's less important that everything included in this library is *consistent* ideologically; instead, there just needs to be a way to find what you need to get the job done. **In other words, the PowerMyth Index is your grandpa's old toolbox.** Goodbye overwhelming hopelessness, hello decentralized avenues for direct action in a unified mission!!

People experience and rationalize the world differently, and that's helpful for us! However, I understand that not everyone, especially these days, sees diversity as a solution for inequity. In fact, sometimes even the smallest shows of diversity (a woke Disney movie casting or whatever) are branded with the scariest possible term man could imagine: [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/31 Mythappropriation/31.01. Cultural Marxism\|Cultural Marxism]]. Well hey, marginalized identities carry incredible insight about how Power (and therefore society) works. But perhaps not one, five, or even ten pieces can complete a puzzle. What about the wealthy?! WHAT ABOUT GAMERS?!??! 

Let's give that toolbox a fresh coat of paint. The PowerMyth Index is organized following an epic framework we like to call... ***NEW CULTURAL MARXISM+***

Understanding [[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]] solely as a means for people to dominate each other is insufficient. Instead...
- Power is a ubiquitous force that determines the boundaries for what every person can or can't do ([[10 - 19 Power/11. Realms of Freedom\|11. Realms of Freedom]]).
- When we come together to govern a society, the views and perceptions of some people are more represented than others ([[10 - 19 Power/12. Dominant Realm\|12. Dominant Realm]]).
- That influence is baked into institutions and culture, which maintains the status quo and reinforces exclusion ([[10 - 19 Power/13. Realm Enforcers\|13. Realm Enforcers]]).
- Those advantaged with certain accumulative properties, such as wealth and status, have greater potential to shape boundaries ([[10 - 19 Power/14. Power Capacitors\|14. Power Capacitors]]).
- An eternal process of [[10 - 19 Power/15. Boundary Negotiation\|15. Boundary Negotiation]] underpins society to determine what is good, possible, normal, legal, or rational.

## Fun Disclaimers
I am not a researcher living in a bubble of pure science and reason, nor is this all intended to be some objectively correct lighthouse for the world's problems. Just because I'm compiling a bunch of perspectives doesn't mean I'm absent one of my own, which also comes with limitations and blind spots. **Expect contradictions!** I will criticize [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/33. Categorization\|Categorization]] despite doing just that, I will question [[40 - 49 Power Conduits/42. Academia\|Academia]] while being part of it, and so on. Maybe two things can be true at once, maybe there's no such thing as truth, idk. But just keep that in mind I guess?

Also, this Index will be **WEIRD** AND **MESSY** FOREVER!!! :O Ideas will keep evolving over time. Expect some pages to be delicately crafted essays while others will just be reference lists for now. Either way, my hope is that each page offers something you can chew on to help reexamine your own experiences.

ALSO! Sometime in the future when I get my life together, the end goal is to turn this project into a collaborative wiki. We need your contributions. We need your disagreements. I just... don't know how to logistically open that up yet?? In the meantime, if you have thoughts, recommendations, or hate mail, please send it my way at belmontindex@gmail.com!
