## Hello!
Thank you for visiting the PowerMyth Index, a digital garden that compiles my original research and helpful source material for studying social inequity. I initially wanted to connect every scattered bit of info related to inequity (causes, solutions, history, etc.) to synthesize it all in plain language. As it turns out, that's just like... everything. Everything in the world is related to inequity!! How do you categorize everything in the world? Well, what you see now is one attempt.

## PowerMyth Machine
Pages on this website are organized and contextualized by the *PowerMyth Machine*. The Machine is a method of conceptualizing social relations through **[[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|10. Power]]**, pulling from a bunch of different theories outlined in the [[00 - 09 Website/04. Bibliography\|04. Bibliography]].

Essentially, PowerMyth is built around the idea that understanding Power solely as a means for people to dominate each other is super insufficient. Instead...
- Power is a ubiquitous force that determines the boundaries for what every person can or can't do ([[10 - 19 Power/11. Realms of Freedom\|11. Realms of Freedom]]).
- When we come together to govern a society, the views and perceptions of some people are more represented than others ([[10 - 19 Power/12. Dominant Realm\|12. Dominant Realm]]).
- That influence is baked into institutions and culture, which maintains the status quo and reinforces exclusion ([[10 - 19 Power/13. Realm Enforcers\|13. Realm Enforcers]]).
- Those advantaged with accumulative properties, such as wealth and status, have greater potential to shape boundaries ([[10 - 19 Power/14. Power Capacitors\|14. Power Capacitors]]).
- An eternal process of [[10 - 19 Power/15. Boundary Negotiation\|15. Boundary Negotiation]] underpins society to determine what is good, possible, normal, legal, or rational.

## Fun Disclaimers
I am not a researcher in a bubble of pure science and reason, nor is this Machine intended to be some objectively correct lighthouse for the world's problems. Instead, this Index will be **WEIRD** AND **MESSY** FOREVER!!! :O Ideas will keep evolving over time, and the scope for the Machine is potentially infinite, I guess. Expect some pages to be delicately crafted essays while others will just be reference lists for now. And expect new pages to keep popping up all the time.

I don't always feel comfortable with 'unfinished' work out there in the wild, but I think it kind of fits with this project. There shouldn't always be a clear argument; this information is here to help examine things differently and apply it to your own experiences. Plus, there's always more to learn.

Oh, and any sociological theory is incomplete unless you know about the guy writing it. Like, it's only fair for the dude who "scientifically proves" that turtles suck to disclose his 20-year tenure as president of the Turtle Haters Club, you know? Yes, this Index is to be grounded in empirical research. However, I also want you to know what influenced my interpretations of the world so you can come to your own conclusions. For even more hypertransparency, I set aside a [[90 Blog/90. Blog\|90. Blog]] section so I can keep some opinion-based writing on display.
