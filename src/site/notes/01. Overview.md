## Welcome to the **PowerMyth Index**

Invisible and otherworldly forces have overwhelming influence on our existence. The economy. Levers of government. Spurious minds of the elite. All are complex entities with their own logics, to be controlled only by the small selection of operators who appear to know best. But what has come of this society shaped by experts and their algorithms, aside from a public too anxious and divided to afford even basic opportunity for the poorest among us? The human experience is far too rich to be contained or represented by any rational order, yet claims of universal truth merely reinforce winners and losers of what should be a collaborative story. 

Only through the mystical can the unseen be known and humanity find harmony. We can reshape our destinies if we uncover that which masquerades as divine will: **[[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]]**. Despite how it may often seem, Power is not exclusively held by those supposed experts who know how to wield it. Power can be generated. The forms it takes on are numerous, however, and we are always partially blinded by **[[20 - 29 Myths/20 Power Myths/20.00. Power Myths\|Myth]]** of our own making. Stories we tell to perceive the world eventually become the unstated, the taken-for-granted, and the assumptions that inform our decision-making. So, only through collective action can we truly comprehend these hidden mechanisms of society.

Individually, we are captured by the influence of what we do not yet know. Together, we can **QUESTION EVERYTHING.**

![powermyth index](https://dillonbelmont.com/03powermyth-assets/PowerMyth.gif)

Uhhhhh okay so basically, a lot of our knowledge gets siloed across specialty and identity in a way that obscures patterns and forsakes our inherent connectedness. That's not good. This website aims to build out Power as a *universal contact language* so that perspectives across fields of study can be in conversation with one another.

Given the absence of an objective reality, this isn't intended to build some single explanation of the world. In fact, research and experiences included in the Index's various reading lists will sometimes be in direct conflict, and that's okay! Take it from the preeminent philosopher of our era:

![sonic everything is canon|453](https://dillonbelmont.com/03powermyth-assets/canon.jpeg)

The context of these ideas is more important than the ideological consistency among them. Ideally, this will leave you with multiple pathways to find what you need to get the job done. Think of the PowerMyth Index as your grandpa's old toolbox, with a little bit of everything mixed in. Goodbye overwhelming hopelessness, hello decentralized avenues for direct action in a unified mission!! Hooray!!

## The Framework

Every page on the Index is written through its relation to [[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]], so it is *highly recommended* to read that section before any others. Just in case though, here is a basic summary of key concepts.

**Power is much more than a means for people to dominate each other. Instead...**
- Power is a ubiquitous force that determines the boundaries of their [[10 - 19 Power/11. Realms of Freedom\|Realm of Freedom]], or what every person can or can't do;
- When we come together to govern a society, the perspectives of some people are more represented than others in the collective [[10 - 19 Power/12. Dominant Realm\|Dominant Realm]];
- Such influence is baked into the social stabilizers of institutions and culture ([[10 - 19 Power/13. Realm Enforcers\|Realm Enforcers]]), which then serve to maintain the status quo and reinforce exclusion;
- Those advantaged with certain accumulative properties, such as wealth and status ([[10 - 19 Power/14. Power Capacitors\|Power Capacitors]]), have a greater potential to shape the boundaries of what is possible; and
- The boundaries of what is possible in society are eternally being [[10 - 19 Power/15. Boundary Negotiation\|Negotiated]] due to the action of all actors.

This Index includes patterns of action that are commonly used to restrict freedoms ([[30 - 39 Power Weapons/30. Power Weapons\|Power Weapons]]) and expand them ([[50 - 59 Power Tools/50. Power Tools\|Power Tools]]), even though the distinction is often fuzzy. The Index also includes two sections on actors: [[40 - 49 Power Conduits/40. Power Conduits\|Power Conduits]] are entities that operate as vessels for Power in unique ways, while [[60 - 69 Power Generators/60. Power Generators\|Power Generators]] are those who mostly exist outside the Dominant Realm. All four sections help us understand the various ways Power can manifest.

Oh, and then there's [[70 - 79 Cyberspace/70. Cyberspace\|Cyberspace]]... Well, stay tuned for that section. Lots of fun things down the pipeline.
