### What is Power?

**Power** is the key to understanding social relations: it influences who we are and what decisions we make. However, this concept isn't quite as intuitive as we might think.  

For today’s public imagination, one interpretation of power conquers all. A businessman *gains power* as he rises through the ranks in the company. An empire *amasses power* as its territory expands. Super Mario even *powers up* when he obtains a mushroom. This intangible concept is often rationalized as a resource that accumulates like capital at the expense of the power*less*. The more you have, the stronger you are. You can choose your own destiny, and perhaps more importantly, dominate others. *But is it really so simple?*

In the form of money or job description, power is easily measurable and compared. But who has more power between an unassuming billionaire and an armed vigilante? Which is more influential between a coordinated propaganda network and a deeply resonant work of art? And is it possible for even a successful CEO to be insulated from racial discrimination or stereotyping? The exchange rate, if there even is one, is entirely context-dependent. And this assumes people know they "have" power and use it accordingly, but is that really true either?

So, there are limits to *exclusively* considering the [[10 - 19 Power/14. Capacities of Power\|14. Capacities of Power]]. It may be a practical starting point, but power is like an iceberg where its visible expressions only represent a small fraction of its possible configurations. Domination happens in other ways, too. A person, or a group, can be excluded from writing the rules that affect them. They can be psychologically manipulated. **BUT WAIT!!** Let me ask you this - is *domination* even enough to describe power?

Just because power is relational by its very nature does not necessarily mean someone is actively exercising it. There are also the [[10 - 19 Power/13. Realm Enforcers\|13. Realm Enforcers]], which can almost be thought of more like... energy. Aura. [Nanomachines, son](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RhMsboqMMzs). Culture, institutions, and their associated norms serve as forces that impact all of our decision-making.

I suppose part of the case I'm trying to make with this website - and in explaining why power literacy is important - is that all of these weird, invisible types of power become more relevant with time. As visible power is questioned, it forces those who wish to dominate into shifting their tactics. Did public backlash of the military draft really stop that practice, or is it now just disguised as "voluntary" recruitment? The tools of [[70 - 79 Cyberspace/70 Cyberspace/70. Cyberspace\|70. Cyberspace]] provide all actors with more possibilities than ever.

Either way, reaching this deep into the well of power gives us a much clearer picture of what a more comprehensive definition looks like. **Power is a network of social boundaries that constrain and enable action for all actors**.[^1]

Power is complex; it is defined by contradictions. Just as it subjugates, it subtly guides. Just as it is driven through legislation, it is driven through conversation. Just as it is acquired by an economic elite, it is built by people living on the street. The more we can understand power, the better equipped we are to harness it for positive social change.

### Uh... Ok, What Next?
Right now, you might be asking, "[[00 - 09 Website/03. WHO CARES!!!!\|03. WHO CARES!!!!]], where is the practical application of this?" Well, if power is indeed everywhere and affects everything... we can use that to understand the world. I'm of the opinion that power literacy gives us an incredibly useful lens. It's not satisfying to talk about systemic issues while ignoring individual accountability, and neither is the inverse focused solely on personal choice. Power helps us understand the cyclical relationship between the two.
