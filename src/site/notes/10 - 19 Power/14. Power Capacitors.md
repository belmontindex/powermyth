**Power Capacitors** are accumulative capital that serve as *potential* mechanisms of [[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]], as either [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/30. Power Weapons\|Weapons]] or [[50 - 59 Power Tools/50. Power Tools\|Tools]]. Capacitors, whether material or otherwise, and be combined in a number of different ways to provide an advantage to actors in the process of [[10 - 19 Power/15. Boundary Negotiation\|Boundary Negotiation]].

![capacitors change to tools](https://dillonbelmont.com/03powermyth-assets/cpct_change.gif)

There are a bajillion different types of Capacitors, all of which have context-sensitive and relationally determined values. Even though they can be (and often are) mixed, there is no clear exchange rate because of that. So, let's get into some different Capacitors first and discuss some generalizations afterward:

## Economic

**Economic capital** allows someone to fulfill many of life’s basic needs, including food, shelter, and transportation. Wealth accumulation, from money to stocks and other assets, is the most tangible Capacitor due to its very clear ability to be quantified.

In a zero-sum economy with finite resources, accumulation of this Capacitor ([[30 - 39 Power Weapons/35. Concentration\|Concentration]]) is at the expense of another ([[30 - 39 Power Weapons/36. Deprivation\|Deprivation]]). And idk, it's tricky when wealth and debt both have inherent properties that further entrench the status of one's economic capital... you know, interest and whatnot. Wealth-generating doors are often inaccessible to those who already lack wealth.

Anyway, here's an interesting website that visualizes disparities: https://realtimeinequality.org/

## Informational

**Informational capital** is access to and utilization of knowledge. Is leveraged most frequently for [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/34. Normalization\|normalization]] and [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/37. Repression\|repression]]. Higher for folks who produce/share knowledge and can set the terms of "truth" for others. Not an exhaustive list but includes:
- Folks who have a wide audience/platform/reach, like the [[40 - 49 Power Conduits/44. Media\|media]] or online [[70 - 79 Cyberspace/73. Weapons_C/73.07. Influence\|influencers]]
- Those with credibility or formal credentials, like [[40 - 49 Power Conduits/42. Academia\|academia]] or [[40 - 49 Power Conduits/41. Government\|government]]
- Authority figures like employers, as described by [[80 - 89 Resources/81 People/81.42. John Gaventa\|Gaventa's]] *Power and Powerlessness.*
- Those who exchange their economic capital for it, like advertisers
- Those who make the rules/algorithms on [[70 - 79 Cyberspace/74. Conduits_C/74.01. Digital Platforms\|digital platforms]]

## Political

**Political capital** is correlated with one's ability to shape [[10 - 19 Power/13. Realm Enforcers#Institutions\|institutions]] through law and regulation.
- To super oversimplify it, imagine it as the President having 1,000 political capital and the average voter having like 1.
- This obviously gets way more complicated when you factor in voter initiatives, interest groups, etc... and how economic capital can *often* translate to more political capital but not always

Since it impacts institutions, it can result in significant changes to the [[10 - 19 Power/12. Dominant Realm\|Dominant Realm]] directly (e.g., X thing is now illegal) or can impact the accumulation of another Capacitor (e.g., X group now pays fewer taxes).
- We can pretty much associate this with any of the institutional effects actually, like formalizing [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/33. Categorization\|categories]] - go see that Realm Enforcers section for more
- It also enables someone to set the agenda, as [[80 - 89 Resources/81 People/81.19. Bachrach & Baratz\|Bachrach & Baratz]] explore, [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/37. Repression\|repressing]] the voices or needs of certain groups... I'll probably talk about voter disenfranchisement on the [[30 - 39 Power Weapons/36. Deprivation\|deprivation]] page, not sure yet.
