**Countercultures** seek to expand [[10 - 19 Power/12. Dominant Realm\|Dominant Realms]] through cultural change, developing systems of norms rooted in fundamental opposition to dominant culture. They often generate [[10 - 19 Power/10. Power\|Power]] by rejecting the notion of people governed by socially constructions they were not consciously involved in the development of. Long before ‘[[60 - 69 Power Generators/61 Heterodoxy/61.06. Hippies\|the Hippie]]’ was reduced to a mass-produced Halloween costume, this identity posed a threat to inequities in American society by **subverting what we understand to be normal.** 

![psychadelic](https://dillonbelmont.com/03powermyth-assets/60/61countercultures.jpg)

Countercultures are sort of a black sheep among the [[60 - 69 Power Generators/60. Power Generators\|Power Generators]]. Unlike those who were already oppressed by (or otherwise existed outside) society's Dominant Realm, members of countercultures typically make that decision on their own. Participation is on a spectrum anyway, so they could potentially have the lower-stakes privilege of returning to comfort at any time.

The general image of countercultures, that of psychedelic experimentation and retreat from society, also probably seems innocuous compared to more explicit forms of political action. Their [[10 - 19 Power/15. Boundary Negotiation\|boundary negotiation]] was not intentionally addressing poverty, racism, or other disparities in [[10 - 19 Power/14. Power Capacitors\|Power Capacitors]]. Even without over-generalizing, I think it's reasonable to suggest their placement in this section is not without controversy!!

Regardless, there is still much to learn from those who critique the Dominant Realm from the inside. Instead of a material focus, countercultural actions often serve to question the resolute supremacy of mainstream institutions and culture.[^1] In some cases, they even develop alternative [[10 - 19 Power/13.
